Ocean

Oceans and seas cover more than two-thirds of the Earth's surface. Amazingly, they contain 97 percent of all the water in the world. The oceans are about four kilometres deep on average, but in some places the ocean floor plunges down even deeper. The deepest place in the world is at bottom of the Pacific ocean - Marianas Trench is more than 11 km below water surface.

Ocean water: There are five oceans, which are joined together by smaller seas and other stretches of water. From largest to smallest, the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Arctic and the Southern.

Moving Water : The water in our oceans and seas is always moving. As wind blows across the water's surface, it creates waves. Waves move around the world in swirling, circular patterns called currents.

Giant Waves: Earthquake under the sea bed can produce giant waves that race towards land at speeds as fast as 970 kilometres an hour .These wave may be 30 metres high near the shore. They are called tsunamis.

Seaweed Harwest: Some farmers grow seaweed. In shallow, tropical waters, people grow their own on plots on the seabed. The harvested seaweed is a useful ingredient in products such as plant fertilizer and ice-cream.

Ocean Life The oceans are filled with living things. These range from the tiny shrimp like creatures that floats on the surface to strange looking fish that crawl across the sea-bed. Most ocean creatures live in the warmer, sunlit waters near the surface, where most of their food supply is found.

Ocean Creatures: Sharks, dolphins and turtles live near the water's surface. Large mammals such as the sei whale dive to lower levels. The waters at the bottom of the ocean receive no sunlight . Only a few kinds of sea creatures such as deep-sea fish and starfish can survive in this cold , dark world.

Floating life: Tiny plants and animals called plankton drift through the surface of the water in huge nimbers. They become food for larger ocean creatures.
